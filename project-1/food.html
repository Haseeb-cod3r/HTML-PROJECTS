These are the dishes we offer our foodies

Chicken Karahi

Chicken Karahi is a dish originating from the northern regions of the Indian subcontinent, and it's a staple in Pakistani cuisine. It's cooked in a karahi (a wok-like cooking vessel), which gives the dish its name. The chicken is cooked in a rich, tomato-based sauce with a blend of spices. The key to a good karahi is the use of fresh tomatoes and a generous amount of ginger and garlic. It's typically served with naan or roti and is known for its bold and spicy flavors.

Biryani

Biryani is a mixed rice dish with its roots in the Mughal cuisine of India. It's a complex and aromatic dish, combining rice, meat (usually chicken, mutton, or beef), and a variety of spices. There are many regional variations of biryani, each with its own unique blend of flavors. Pakistani biryani, especially Sindhi and Hyderabadi biryanis, are known for their rich use of spices and aromatic basmati rice. The meat is often marinated and cooked separately before being layered with the rice and other ingredients. Biryani is a celebratory dish, often served at special occasions and festivals.

Nihari

Nihari is a slow-cooked stew, traditionally made with beef or mutton, and it's considered a culinary treasure in Pakistan. It's a breakfast dish, often enjoyed with naan. The meat is cooked for several hours until it becomes incredibly tender and the broth develops a rich, gelatinous consistency. Nihari is flavored with a special blend of spices, including a unique nihari masala. It's a hearty and flavorful dish, perfect for a cold morning. It's a testament to the art of slow cooking and the rich culinary traditions of Pakistan.

Chicken Tikka Masala

While its exact origins are debated (some say it originated in a Glasgow, Scotland Indian restaurant), Chicken Tikka Masala is undeniably a beloved dish worldwide. It features marinated chicken pieces that are first grilled or baked, then added to a creamy, tomato-based sauce. The sauce is typically rich and flavorful, featuring a blend of spices like garam masala, coriander, and turmeric. It's often served with naan bread, perfect for soaking up the delicious sauce. In Pakistan, it's a popular restaurant dish and also made at home.

chiken karahai

ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces

  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste

  • 2 large onions, finely chopped

  • 4 tomatoes, pureed

  • 1 tsp turmeric powder

  • 1 tsp red chili powder

instructions

  1. Heat oil in a karahi (wok) and add onions. Sauté until golden brown

  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a minute.

  3. Add chicken and cook until it is browned on all sides.

  4. Add tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 10 minutes, or until the oil starts to separate.

  5. Add garam masala and mix well.
