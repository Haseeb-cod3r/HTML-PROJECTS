COLOURS

Periodic tables are often color-coded to visually represent the different groups or families of elements. Elements in the same group tend to share similar chemical properties due to having the same number of valence electrons (electrons in their outermost shell).

Alkali Earth

The red colour group are Alkali metals (Group 1) are highly reactive, soft, silvery metals. They have one valence electron, making them eager to react, especially with water. They're never found pure in nature.

Alkaline Metal

The pink colour group are Alkaline earth metals (Group 2) are reactive metals with two valence electrons. They're less reactive than alkali metals, form +2 ions, and aren't found pure in nature.

Transition Metal

The yellow colour group are Transition metals (Groups 3-12) are dense, conductive, and can have multiple oxidation states. They form colorful compounds and are vital in industry and biology.

Basic Metal

The purpul colour group are Basic metals (post-transition metals) are softer, lower-melting metals in the p-block. They're less reactive than alkali/alkaline earth metals, but more than transition metals, and show some covalent bonding.

Metalloid

The blue colour group are Metalloids are elements with properties between metals and nonmetals. They're brittle, semi-conductive, and crucial in electronics (e.g., silicon).

Non Metal

The orange colour group are Nonmetals lack metallic properties, are poor conductors, and gain electrons in reactions. They exist as gases, liquids, or solids at room temperature.

Noble Gas

The light green colour group are Noble gases are inert, colorless gases with full outer electron shells, making them stable and unreactive.

Lanthanoid

The light blue colour group are Lanthanoids are 15 metallic elements (atomic numbers 57-71) with similar chemical properties due to filling the 4f subshell, used in alloys and electronics.

Actinoid

the green colour group are Actinoids are 15 radioactive elements (atomic numbers 89-103) filling the 5f subshell, used in nuclear applications.
